## Humor Style

### Light & Self-Aware
We don't take ourselves too seriously. Humor is gentle, never sarcastic or exclusionary.

**Examples:**

## Language Specifics

### Sentence Structure
- **Short, punchy sentences** for CTAs and navigation
- **Medium, flowing sentences** for descriptions and explanations
- **Longer, poetic sentences** for philosophy and mission statements

### Vocabulary Choices
**Prefer:**
- Active voice: "Add to the Story" (not "Story can be added")
- Direct address: "You" and "Your"
- Present tense: "You're on fire!" (not "You were on fire!")
- Conversational contractions: "It's" "You're" "We're"

**Avoid:**
- Corporate jargon: "utilize," "leverage," "synergy"
- Passive constructions: "is being processed"
- Overly formal: "Upon completion" â†’ "When you're done"

### Key Terminology (Maintain Consistency)
- **LightSaver** (capital L, capital S) - never "light saver" or "lightsaver"
- **lighter** (lowercase) - the physical object
- **Lighter Babies** - when personifying lighters collectively
- **mosaic** - our community's collective stories
- **journey** - a lighter's story over time
- **PIN** (all caps) - the unique code

## Cultural Sensitivity

### Global Perspective
Our community spans 22+ languages and cultures. Translations should:
- Avoid region-specific idioms that don't translate
- Respect cultural differences in formality levels
- Consider reading direction (RTL languages like Arabic, Farsi)
- Maintain environmental message universally

### Inclusive Language
- Use gender-neutral language
- Avoid assumptions about age, location, or lifestyle
- Welcome all participation levels (first-time user to power user)

## Specific Content Types

### 1. Success Messages
- **Tone**: Celebratory, warm, encouraging
- **Length**: 1-2 sentences
- **Formula**: Congratulate + Explain impact + (Optional emoji)
- **Example**: "Story added! ğŸ‰ You just made this lighter's journey a little brighter."

### 2. Error Messages
- **Tone**: Apologetic, helpful, never blaming
- **Length**: 1 sentence problem + 1 sentence solution
- **Formula**: Acknowledge issue + Provide fix + Stay friendly
- **Example**: "Oops! Looks like the internet hiccupped. Check your connection and try again. ğŸŒ"

### 3. CTAs (Calls-to-Action)
- **Tone**: Inviting, action-oriented, exciting
- **Length**: 2-5 words max
- **Formula**: Verb + Benefit
- **Examples**: "Become a LightSaver," "Add to Story," "Save Your First Lighter"

### 4. Empty States
- **Tone**: Hopeful, forward-looking, motivating
- **Length**: 1 sentence observation + 1 sentence invitation
- **Example**: "This lighter's story is waiting to be written! Be the first to add a post and start its journey."

### 5. Educational Content
- **Tone**: Clear, practical, empowering
- **Length**: As needed for clarity
- **Formula**: State benefit + Simple steps + Reassure
- **Example**: "Flip it over. Do you see a small, round metal valve in the center, similar to a tire valve? That's a refill port! Congratulations, your lighter is refillable."

### 6. Philosophy/Mission
- **Tone**: Poetic, passionate, thought-provoking
- **Length**: Longer, flowing paragraphs
- **Formula**: Challenge assumption + Paint vision + Invite participation
- **Example**: "By giving a lighter a permanent, digital story, we give it sentimental value. Your lighter becomes a living artifact, a message in a bottle. And when something has value, we're less likely to throw it away."

## Final Notes for AI Translation

**DO:**
- Maintain the emotional arc of each message
- Adapt idioms to culturally equivalent expressions
- Keep sentences short and scannable
- Preserve the brand's optimistic, community-focused spirit
- Use local humor styles where appropriate

**DON'T:**
- Translate literally if it loses warmth/clarity
- Add corporate formality
- Remove personality to be "safe"
- Ignore cultural context in favor of word-for-word translation
- Sacrifice clarity for brevity
